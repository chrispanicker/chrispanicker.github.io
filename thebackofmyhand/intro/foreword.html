Foreword

I recall a moment in my childhood,
or many moments,
when I stared at myself in the mirror.
I held my hands up, palms forward,
to remind myself of my humanity.

To clarify, it is not inhuman to be brown,
but it certainly felt that way sometimes.

Lastly, I have never experienced overt racism.
Recognizing this, I can never imagine how inhuman
how others feel in such hostile environments.
